Tacos

Description

Tacos are a traditional Mexican dish consisting of a folded or rolled tortilla filled with a variety of ingredients. Common fillings include seasoned meat such as beef, chicken, or pork, along with beans, cheese, lettuce, and salsa. Tacos can be topped with guacamole, sour cream, and fresh lime juice for added flavor. They are versatile, delicious, and can be easily customized to suit different tastes, making them a popular choice for meals and snacks worldwide.

Ingredients

  • 1 pound ground beef or chicken
  • 1 packet taco seasoning mix
  • 2/3 cup water
  • 8 small tortillas (corn or flour)
  • 1 cup shredded lettuce
  • 1 cup diced tomatoes
  • 1/2 cup shredded cheese (cheddar or Mexican blend)
  • 1/4 cup diced onions
  • 1/4 cup chopped fresh cilantro
  • Salsa or hot sauce (optional)
  • Guacamole (optional)
  • Sour cream (optional)
  • Lime wedges (optional)

Steps to make the recipe

    1. Heat a large skillet over medium-high heat. Add the ground beef or chicken and cook until browned, breaking it apart with a spoon as it cooks. Drain any excess fat.
    2. Stir in the taco seasoning mix and water. Bring to a boil, then reduce heat and simmer for about 5 minutes, stirring occasionally, until the sauce thickens.
    3. While the meat is cooking, warm the tortillas in a dry skillet over medium heat, or wrap them in foil and heat in a 350°F (175°C) oven for about 10 minutes.
    4. Assemble the tacos by placing a portion of the seasoned meat on each tortilla.
    5. Top with shredded lettuce, diced tomatoes, shredded cheese, diced onions, and chopped cilantro.
    6. Add salsa or hot sauce, guacamole, and sour cream if desired.
    7. Serve with lime wedges on the side for an extra burst of flavor. Enjoy!
